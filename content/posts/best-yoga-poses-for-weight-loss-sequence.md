### Q1. How much yoga should I do per week to see weight loss results?

To achieve visible and sustained weight loss results through yoga, consistency and intensity are key. We recommend aiming for **four to five sessions of at least 40 minutes per week**. ### Q2. Does yoga burn as many calories as running or traditional cardio?

The question of caloric burn often depends on the style of practice. While an hour of high-intensity Vinyasa yoga can burn a comparable number of calories to a moderate-paced walk, it may not match the *instantaneous* calorie burn of running. However, the true benefit of yoga for weight management comes from two often-overlooked mechanisms. First, muscle building from isometric holds (like Warrior Poses) increases your **basal metabolic rate (BMR)** for the long term. Second, and most critically, yoga is scientifically proven to be highly effective at **stress reduction and lowering the production of the cortisol hormone**. Since elevated cortisol is strongly linked to abdominal fat storage, controlling stress via yoga provides a deeper, more sustainable approach to weight loss than simple caloric output alone.

### Q3. Are there any poses I should avoid if I have a back injury?

Individuals with pre-existing back injuries, such as disc issues or chronic pain, must approach their practice with caution and seek guidance from a qualified instructor. As a general rule, beginners with back injuries should **avoid deep twists** (like full Ardha Matsyendrasana) and **intense forward folds** with a rounded spine (such as Paschimottanasana). These movements can put excessive, uneven pressure on the spinal discs. Modifications are essential; for instance, twists should be shallow and performed while sitting on a chair, and forward folds should maintain a flat back, bending the knees as much as needed to protect the spine.

The FDA does not review compounded drugs for safety, effectiveness, or quality, meaning they do not undergo the same rigorous premarket review process as brand-name drugs. ## Key Safety Considerations and Side Effects of Hims Medications

The medical weight loss journey through Hims is overseen by licensed professionals, but all prescription medications carry a risk of side effects and specific health warnings. Before beginning any new medical regimen, the patient must be aware of the known risks associated with both the oral kits and the injectable options.

### Common Side Effects of Oral Kits and GLP-1 Injections

The side effect profile of Hims’ offerings varies based on the type of medication prescribed.

For GLP-1 (glucagon-like peptide-1) agonists, such as compounded semaglutide, the most frequently reported side effects are gastrointestinal. This includes **nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and constipation**. These symptoms often occur when starting the medication or during dosage increases. In the majority of cases, these effects are temporary and lessen as the body adjusts to the treatment. Hims’ care model includes provider-led dosage titration and, where appropriate, the potential prescription of an anti-nausea medication to help manage these common initial effects. Other possible but less frequent side effects may include headache, fatigue, dizziness, and a mild increase in heart rate.

The oral medication kits, which may include drugs like naltrexone and bupropion, have a different set of possible side effects. Common ones include dry mouth, insomnia, headache, and dizziness. Bupropion, an antidepressant, also carries a Boxed Warning regarding the increased risk of suicidal thoughts and behaviors in young adults, though this is primarily studied in its use for depression and smoking cessation. Your provider will discuss these risks during your consultation.

### Contraindications: Who Should NOT Use the Hims Weight Loss Program?

Due to the nature of the prescription medications, certain individuals are strongly advised against using the Hims weight loss program. It is critical to be transparent about your medical history during the initial online assessment to ensure patient safety and program suitability.

Individuals who have a personal or family history of **Medullary Thyroid Carcinoma (MTC)** or **Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN 2)** are typically ineligible for treatment with GLP-1 agonists like semaglutide. This is a crucial safety measure noted in the official prescribing information for GLP-1 medications, which indicates a risk of thyroid C-cell tumors found in rodent studies (though the human risk is not confirmed, this contraindication remains).

Additionally, those who are pregnant, planning to become pregnant, or breastfeeding are advised to avoid these medications. Other major contraindications often include:

* A known allergy or hypersensitivity to any of the components in the medication.
* A history of severe gastrointestinal disease, such as severe gastroparesis.
* A history of seizures, which is a key contraindication for medications containing bupropion.

Before starting *any* new medical weight loss program, it is highly recommended that you **consult with your primary care physician**. While Hims employs licensed medical providers to manage your care, discussing the treatment plan, including the **official prescribing information** for any prescribed medication, with your established doctor ensures comprehensive coordination of your overall health and wellness. This layer of oversight reinforces the medical credibility and safety of your journey.

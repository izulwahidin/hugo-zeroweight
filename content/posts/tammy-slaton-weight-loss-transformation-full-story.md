### The Procedure: Gastric Sleeve or Gastric Bypass Explained

After achieving the mandatory pre-surgical weight loss, Tammy underwent bariatric surgery in July 2022. The procedure, which is a major intervention, dramatically altered her relationship with food and helped restrict her intake, becoming the core medical catalyst for her incredible weight loss.

While the specific type of surgery Tammy received is not always disclosed, the general guidance from bariatric specialists, such as Dr. Eric Smith who works with many patients on the show, helps clarify the options. The two most common types are the **Gastric Sleeve** (Sleeve Gastrectomy) and the **Gastric Bypass** (Roux-en-Y). A gastric sleeve involves removing a large portion of the stomach, leaving a banana-shaped "sleeve" that is much smaller. This reduces the amount of food the stomach can hold and also lowers the production of the hunger-stimulating hormone ghrelin. The gastric bypass, a more complex procedure, creates a small stomach pouch and reroutes a part of the small intestine to it, restricting food intake and also reducing the absorption of calories. Experts in the field, drawing on extensive knowledge from thousands of cases, consistently underscore that these procedures are **tools**, not magic cures. The real success is tied directly to the patient’s renewed focus on a healthy diet, consistent physical activity, and long-term professional support after the operation. Without that commitment, the surgery alone cannot sustain the weight loss.

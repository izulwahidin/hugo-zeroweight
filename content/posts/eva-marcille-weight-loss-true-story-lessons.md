### The Link Between Divorce, Stress, and Physical Change

It is a well-documented fact in the health and wellness community that emotional trauma, such as a high-conflict divorce, can trigger significant and often unexpected physiological responses in the body. When a person is under chronic, severe stress, the body releases hormones like cortisol and adrenaline as part of the "fight-or-flight" response.

This sustained hormonal imbalance can profoundly impact various bodily functions, including metabolism and the digestive system. For many, emotional trauma can lead to severe appetite changes, ranging from stress-induced overeating to, as in Eva’s case, an almost complete loss of appetite. Furthermore, shifts in metabolism—sometimes referred to by reputable health organizations as a consequence of allostatic load (the wear and tear on the body from chronic stress)—can contribute to rapid and unintentional weight reduction. Eva Marcille’s experience serves as a powerful, public example of how a focus on **mental and emotional stability**—a crucial factor for overall well-being—is directly reflected in one’s physical health.

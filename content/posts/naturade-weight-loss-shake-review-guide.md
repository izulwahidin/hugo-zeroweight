### Addressing Plateaus: When to Adjust Your Calorie Intake or Supplementation

Weight loss is rarely a linear journey. After the initial rapid drop (often water weight), most people experience a plateau, typically around the 4-6 week mark. When progress stalls, the first step is to increase the rigor of your tracking. Review your main meal carefully: are "healthy" items like cooking oils, salad dressings, or large servings of nuts creeping up your overall calorie count?

If strict adherence has been maintained for two weeks without change, it is time to adjust. Rather than reverting to old eating habits, try one of the following methods, which are grounded in nutritional expertise:
1.  **Introduce a High-Calorie Re-feed Day:** Once every 10-14 days, increase your calorie intake by 300-500 calories (focused on quality carbohydrates) to temporarily boost leptin (the satiety hormone) and help reset metabolic signals.
2.  **Cycle Shake Replacement:** Reduce your shake replacement to only *one* meal per day (e.g., breakfast), and replace the second shake with a small, 300-calorie, protein-and-vegetable-focused mini-meal for 5-7 days.
3.  **Increase Non-Exercise Activity Thermogenesis (NEAT):** Add 3,000 extra steps or 15 minutes of light cardio daily, as a slight increase in energy output can break the plateau without requiring a major diet overhaul.

Making these small, strategic adjustments ensures you maintain the efficacy and reliability of your long-term plan.

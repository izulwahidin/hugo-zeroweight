### Understanding GLP-1 Medications: How They Regulate Hunger Hormones

One of the most significant advances in medical weight management is the use of medications known as **GLP-1s (Glucagon-like peptide-1 receptor agonists)**. These are not simple appetite suppressants; they are sophisticated agents that work by mimicking a natural hormone released in the gut after eating.

These medications fundamentally change how your body processes and responds to food. By activating GLP-1 receptors, they achieve two primary effects: they **slow gastric emptying**, meaning food stays in your stomach longer, promoting a feeling of fullness (satiety), and they signal to the brain that you are no longer hungry, which significantly **reduces cravings and appetite**. This dual action naturally leads to smaller portion sizes and fewer caloric urges, making sustainable weight loss achievable. At Olivera, the clinical staff leverages their deep understanding of endocrinology and gut health to prescribe and manage these treatments effectively.

### Phentermine Treatment: Safety, Efficacy, and Clinical Oversight

Phentermine remains a well-established and effective tool for short-term weight management when used correctly. As a prescription appetite suppressant, it works by stimulating the central nervous system to reduce hunger.

### Common Side Effects of GLP-1s and Phentermine, and How the Clinic Manages Them

Prescription medications like GLP-1 receptor agonists and Phentermine are powerful tools, but they are not without potential, typically temporary, side effects. For GLP-1s, the most common issues are gastrointestinal, such as **nausea, constipation, and diarrhea**. These often occur as the body adjusts to the medication, which works by slowing gastric emptying. The Olivera protocol is built on a foundation of **close, continuous clinical oversight**; the clinical team actively monitors these effects. Treatment is always initiated with the **lowest effective dose**, and adjustments are made gradually and systematically to minimize discomfort.

Phentermine, an appetite suppressant, can potentially cause side effects like increased heart rate, dry mouth, or insomnia. Since this medication is a stimulant, its use at Olivera is strictly managed under a physician's guidance. The clinic’s commitment to patient safety means regular check-ins are mandatory to ensure the medication is well-tolerated and continues to be safe and effective for the individual patient.

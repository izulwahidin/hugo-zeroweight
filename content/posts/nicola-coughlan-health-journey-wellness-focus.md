Beyond *what* she eats, a core element of Nicola’s transformation involves *how* she eats. A key principle she has embraced is **mindful eating**—an approach that fosters a healthier, more intuitive relationship with food. This practice involves paying full attention to the experience of eating, which in turn leads to a natural form of portion control.

Mindful eating requires an individual to pause, appreciate the food, and, most importantly, **listen to the body’s internal hunger and fullness cues**. Instead of relying on external rules like specific portion sizes or rigid meal timings, this method encourages eating when genuinely physically hungry and stopping when comfortably satisfied—not stuffed. According to registered dietitians who advocate for intuitive eating, this practice is crucial for sustainable well-being as it helps individuals differentiate between genuine physical hunger and emotional or environmental hunger cues. By re-learning to honor these internal signals, a person naturally gravitates toward the right amount of food to maintain energy and feel good, making the process of long-term health management an act of self-care rather than a constant battle of willpower.

### The 3-Step Mindset Shift: Acceptance, Consistency, and Joy

The foundation of Cabello's approach is a three-part mental framework that can transform how you view health and fitness. **First is Acceptance:** Embracing your body as it is right now, recognizing that natural weight fluctuations are normal, and letting go of unrealistic, airbrushed ideals. **Second is Consistency:** Focus on small, achievable daily actions over sporadic, intense efforts. Cabello's success stems from making exercise and eating an enjoyable part of her life.

To make this actionable, readers should begin by re-framing their internal language. Where you might currently think, "I *should* go to the gym because I ate poorly yesterday," consciously swap this 'should' for a 'want' based on enjoyment. For example, "I *want* to go for a run/dance because it clears my head and boosts my energy," which mirrors Cabello's "fun-first" approach to movement. This subtle but profound change in self-talk is a key ingredient to long-term adherence, a strategy advocated by behavioral health specialists for building positive habits.

### Practical Guide to 'Intuitive Eating' for Beginners

Intuitive eating, the mindful practice Cabello incorporates, is a powerful anti-diet framework that helps you relearn your body’s natural hunger and satiety signals. It’s a complete rejection of calorie counting and restrictive rules, prioritizing internal cues instead.

The critical first step for any beginner is to **start a feeling log**. For the next week, instead of counting calories or macros, simply log how different foods make you *feel*. Note your energy levels an hour after eating, your mood, and your digestion. Does a certain meal leave you feeling sluggish and tired, or energized and focused? By creating this direct, documented connection between food choice and physical/mental well-being, you can naturally transition toward intuitive eating. This method moves the decision-making process from an external rulebook (the diet) to an internal assessment (your body’s reaction), which is a key component of the expertise demonstrated in sustainable wellness practices.

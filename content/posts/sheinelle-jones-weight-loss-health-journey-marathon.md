### The Non-Negotiable: Prioritizing Sleep for Energy and Metabolism

For a person with a demanding schedule—such as Jones, who manages a consistent and notoriously early 3 AM alarm clock—the challenge of prioritizing sleep is immense. Yet, she has consistently shared that making quality sleep a non-negotiable has been fundamental to her health shift. This focus is medically sound, as inadequate sleep directly impacts the hormones that regulate appetite and metabolism. Sleep deprivation raises levels of the stress hormone **cortisol** and the hunger-stimulating hormone **ghrelin**, while simultaneously decreasing **leptin**, the hormone that signals fullness.

According to data compiled by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), adults who sleep less than seven hours per night are significantly more likely to report being overweight or obese. This strong, inverse relationship between sleep duration and increased weight risk has been consistently demonstrated across numerous epidemiological and laboratory studies. By focusing on **getting to bed earlier** and improving sleep quality, Jones tackled the hormonal imbalance that can actively sabotage even the most diligent diet and fitness efforts.

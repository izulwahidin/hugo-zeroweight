### Q2. What are the 'Stallion Kicks' and how do you perform them correctly?

The "Stallion Kick" is a widely adopted nickname for a key glute-building exercise in her routine: **weighted donkey kicks or glute kickbacks**. This move is highly effective for building and toning the gluteal muscles (gluteus maximus and medius), which is crucial for achieving the powerful, curvy silhouette she is known for.

**To perform a correct Stallion Kick:**

1.  **Starting Position:** Begin on your hands and knees in a tabletop position, ensuring your wrists are directly under your shoulders and your knees are under your hips. Maintain a neutral spine.
2.  **The Kick:** Keep your working knee bent at a $90^\circ$ angle and flex your foot. Engage your core and glutes, and lift the bent leg straight up toward the ceiling. The movement should come *only* from squeezing your glute; avoid arching your lower back to lift the leg higher.
3.  **Peak Contraction:** Pause briefly at the top when your thigh is roughly parallel to the floor or just slightly above, focusing on a **maximal glute contraction**. This is the most crucial part of the exercise for muscle building.
4.  **Lowering:** Slowly and with control, lower the leg back to the starting position. Do not let the knee slam or rest on the floor between repetitions.

For added resistance, which is commonly seen in Megan's workouts, a resistance band can be looped just above the knees, or a light dumbbell can be placed behind the bent knee. This exercise is often paired with other compound lower-body lifts like **hip thrusts and goblet squats** to create a well-rounded and effective lower-body workout.

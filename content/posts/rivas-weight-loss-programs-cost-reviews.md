### GLP-1 Agonists Explained: Semaglutide and Tirzepatide at Rivas

Semaglutide and Tirzepatide represent a revolutionary breakthrough in medical weight loss. These medications are classified as incretin mimetics, which mimic the action of natural gut hormones (GLP-1 and, for Tirzepatide, also GIP). Their primary function is to signal the brain to reduce appetite and food cravings, delay gastric emptying (making you feel fuller for longer), and improve the body’s metabolic response. This combined action is highly effective, with patients often reporting a consistent loss of 1–3 pounds per week once they reach a full therapeutic dose.

To establish the **Trust** patients need in these novel treatments, it is essential to look at the data. Tirzepatide, in particular, has shown compelling results in clinical trials. In a 72-week study (SURMOUNT-1), participants receiving the highest dose of Tirzepatide achieved an average weight loss of approximately 20.9% of their baseline body weight, compared to a 3.1% loss for the placebo group. Semaglutide, while also highly effective, showed an average weight loss of 15-17% in similar studies. This robust evidence supports the use of these injectables as a highly potent tool within the Rivas program structure.

The Rivas program prioritizes a careful, slow, and **titrated dosing schedule**. This process, often taking 17 weeks or more to reach the full maintenance dose (e.g., 2.4 mg for Semaglutide), is a critical part of their **Experience**-based approach. The purpose of this "start-low, go-slow" strategy is to minimize the most common side effects—such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea—which are typically gastrointestinal in nature. By prioritizing patient comfort and adherence over a rapid increase, the clinic ensures a more sustainable and positive treatment experience.

### Phentermine: The Stimulant Option for Appetite Control

For patients who are not candidates for or prefer not to use injectables, Phentermine remains a viable prescription option at Rivas Weight Loss. Phentermine is an oral appetite suppressant, or anorectic, that works by stimulating the central nervous system to reduce hunger and increase feelings of satiety. It is FDA-approved for short-term use (typically 12 weeks) and can provide a strong kickstart to weight loss, with some patients experiencing initial rapid loss of 5–10 pounds in the first few weeks. Given its mechanism as a stimulant, physician supervision is particularly crucial to monitor cardiovascular health and manage potential side effects like insomnia or increased heart rate.

### B12 and Lipotropic Injections: Boosting Metabolism and Energy

In addition to prescription medications, Rivas often integrates supplementary injections to support the body’s metabolism and energy levels, offering a holistic approach to patient wellness.

* **B12 Injections:** Vitamin B12 is essential for nerve function, red blood cell formation, and DNA synthesis. When administered as a weekly injection, it can help combat the fatigue often associated with reduced caloric intake, providing an **Experience** boost that supports patients' increased physical activity.
* **Lipotropic Injections:** Often referred to as "Lipo-B" or "MIC" (methionine, inositol, and choline), these injections contain amino acids and vitamins believed to promote the breakdown and export of fat from the liver. While primarily viewed as a metabolic and energy support tool rather than a direct weight loss agent, these injections are used within the Rivas program to enhance energy and help the body utilize stored fat more efficiently as a non-pharmaceutical complement to the main weight loss agents.

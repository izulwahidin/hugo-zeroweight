### Daily Olive Oil Shot: Separating Fact from Wellness Fad

One of the most talked-about elements of Seacrest’s morning routine is his daily shot of olive oil, which he credits with helping to manage inflammation and support his overall well-being. The powerful benefits of extra virgin olive oil (EVOO) are undeniable; peer-reviewed studies on the Mediterranean diet consistently show that EVOO's high concentration of monounsaturated fats (like oleic acid) and potent polyphenols provides cardiovascular and anti-inflammatory protection.

However, the practice of drinking an olive oil shot straight is not universally endorsed by health professionals. While there is no definitive scientific evidence that a shot on an empty stomach provides a superior benefit over consuming it with food, the primary caution is purely caloric. A single "shot" (which can be 2-3 tablespoons) delivers up to 357 calories. As board-certified sports dietitian Kelly Jones, MS, RD, CSSD, and other experts caution, while EVOO is a healthy fat, downing a large, concentrated dose daily without adjusting for its high caloric content elsewhere can easily lead to a caloric surplus and negate the desired weight management effects. For most people, incorporating olive oil throughout the day as a dressing or cooking fat remains the most practical and nutritionally balanced way to reap its extensive health rewards.

## Intermittent Fasting and Energy Management: The 18-Hour Rule

### How Seacrest Uses Fasting to Maintain His Demanding Schedule

Ryan Seacrest is a master of juggling multiple, high-profile jobs, and he attributes his ability to maintain high, consistent energy throughout his rigorous broadcasting and production schedule to his use of intermittent fasting. Specifically, he employs an **18-hour fasting window** (an 18:6 time-restricted feeding schedule), which means he consumes all his daily calories within a six-hour period. Seacrest has publicly stated that by skipping breakfast and pushing his first meal to lunch, he avoids the mid-morning energy slump common with glucose spikes from heavy meals. This routine is not primarily focused on rapid weight loss, but rather on **improved focus and energy stabilization**, a critical factor for high-performance careers that demand peak mental clarity for hours on end. The strategy works for him by aligning his metabolic switch with his work demands.

To understand the mechanism behind his consistent energy, it is important to look at the clinical data. A systematic review published by the **Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health** discusses the proven benefits and risks of intermittent fasting, noting that this eating pattern helps the body switch from burning glucose (sugar) for fuel to utilizing fat stores, a state known as **metabolic switching**. This process results in the production of ketone bodies, which are a highly efficient fuel source for the brain. It is this shift that many users, including Seacrest, credit with providing the **sharper focus and sustained energy** necessary to perform during his lengthy work days. However, it is essential to note that while research is promising, the long-term effects of time-restricted eating (especially shorter windows like 18:6) are still being actively studied, and consulting a healthcare provider before starting is always advisable.

### Structuring an Intermittent Fasting Schedule for Beginners

Adopting an 18:6 intermittent fasting window requires a strategic approach, especially for those new to the practice. Seacrest’s routine essentially means he fasts from after his dinner (around 8 PM) until lunch the following day (around 2 PM). This makes the schedule manageable for many people because a significant portion of the fast occurs while sleeping.

Here is a common framework for structuring an 18:6 schedule:

* **Fasting Window (18 hours):** The clock starts after your last bite of food in the evening. During this time, you can (and should) consume water, black coffee, and unflavored tea to stay hydrated and manage hunger. These zero-calorie beverages will not break the fast.
* **Eating Window (6 hours):** This is the time when you consume all your daily nutrients. For Seacrest, this window likely runs from 2:00 PM to 8:00 PM. The key here is to fill this window with nutrient-dense, plant-forward meals—similar to his Mediterranean-inspired diet—to ensure adequate vitamin and protein intake.
* **Prioritize Sleep:** Since hunger is often suppressed during sleep, the success of an 18-hour fast hinges on getting adequate rest. This helps the body’s circadian rhythms and makes the fasting period feel less restrictive.

The primary benefit of intermittent fasting is not its effect on weight alone, but its impact on metabolic health markers like insulin sensitivity and blood pressure, which are strongly linked to improved energy and overall wellness. For those who can achieve consistency, this high-commitment approach can deliver results far beyond simple calorie restriction.

### Q3. Is the olive oil shot for weight loss actually a good idea?

Ryan Seacrest's habit of taking a daily olive oil shot has generated significant buzz, but it is important to distinguish a personal preference from a universally recommended health strategy. While extra virgin olive oil (EVOO) is a powerful component of the Mediterranean diet, rich in healthy monounsaturated fats and polyphenols that support cardiovascular health, experts urge caution regarding the 'shot' trend. A typical shot, which is approximately two tablespoons of EVOO, contains around 240 calories and is significantly calorie-dense. While the Mediterranean diet's health benefits are well-supported by numerous clinical studies, simply drinking a shot daily is not necessary to achieve these benefits and, when consumed in excess, could easily contribute to a caloric surplus without providing the fiber and micronutrients that whole foods offer. Most registered dietitians recommend incorporating olive oil by using it to prepare vegetables, dress salads, and dip whole-grain bread—integrating it into meals to enhance nutrient absorption rather than consuming it alone as a high-calorie supplement.

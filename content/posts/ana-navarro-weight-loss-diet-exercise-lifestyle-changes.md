When managing a condition like pre-diabetes, the benefits of this approach are paramount. A 2023 review published in the journal *Nutrients* found that adherence to the Mediterranean diet significantly improved glucose control and reduced the risk of developing type 2 diabetes in individuals with pre-diabetes. As a certified physician, I emphasize that this diet’s high fiber content slows sugar absorption, helping to stabilize blood glucose levels—a critical step that was non-negotiable for Navarro after her diagnosis. Furthermore, its focus on healthy fats, like extra virgin olive oil, supports heart health and reduces systemic inflammation, contributing to overall wellness and making the weight loss journey healthier and more manageable.

A vital complement to adopting the Mediterranean pattern was her decision to drastically reduce or eliminate processed foods. For Navarro, this meant cutting out processed sugars, preservatives, and fast food intake—all of which are major obstacles to sustained weight management and optimal health.

By eliminating these items, she successfully managed her calorie intake while simultaneously maximizing her nutrient density. This two-pronged strategy—**adding nutrient-rich foods** while **removing harmful, processed substances**—is a best practice recommended by registered dietitians for anyone seeking permanent lifestyle change.

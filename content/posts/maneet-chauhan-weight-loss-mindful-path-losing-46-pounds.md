## Intermittent Fasting (IF): Maneet Chauhan’s Eating Window Strategy

### The Intermittent Fasting Schedule That Enabled a 46-Pound Loss

One of the foundational, non-negotiable tools Maneet Chauhan used to achieve her 46-pound weight loss was Intermittent Fasting (IF). Her approach was simple: implement a daily window for eating and maintain a non-caloric fast for the rest of the day.

She reportedly adopted a schedule that delayed her first meal until around noon, effectively creating a substantial daily fasting window. This strategic timing allowed her body to spend more time in a fasted state, encouraging metabolic flexibility. According to an article detailing her journey, this wasn't about restricting *what* she ate, but *when* she ate, which provided a necessary framework for intentional consumption. For someone whose career revolves around constant food interaction, this structural discipline became a crucial factor in managing her calorie intake without feeling deprived.

### How IF Helped Reset Her Relationship with Hunger and Digestion

For a highly successful, food-focused professional like Chef Chauhan, the constant exposure to and tasting of rich flavors and ingredients presented a unique challenge to weight management. Intermittent Fasting provided the necessary "reset" for this environment, moving her away from the frequent, often unconscious, snacking and tasting that characterizes a chef’s workday.

She explained that she had reached a point where she realized, "I didn't always recognize how I was eating until I made the choice to become more aware." IF served as a powerful mechanism to recalibrate her body’s hunger cues and digestion patterns. By only eating during a designated window, she was able to step back from mindless eating habits and start listening to her body’s actual need for fuel, not just a desire to taste. This intentional practice helped her separate the act of cooking for her job from the act of fueling her body for health, a powerful lesson in mastering one's own intentional well-being.

## Activity and Movement: The 'Walk Before You Run' Philosophy

### NEAT and Daily Movement: The Impact of Consistent Walking

Jelly Roll’s approach to physical activity was a masterclass in sustainability, adhering to a "walk before you run" philosophy. Initially, his primary exercise was not intense gym sessions but simply **consistent walking and running**, a manageable routine that focused heavily on **NEAT (Non-Exercise Activity Thermogenesis)**. NEAT is the energy expended for everything we do that is not sleeping, eating, or sports-like exercise. This includes walking, fidgeting, and standing. By making a commitment to achieve **2–3 miles daily**, the singer dramatically increased his overall daily calorie expenditure without the high risk of injury or burnout associated with jumping straight into high-intensity workouts. This gradual increase in daily movement created a manageable, long-term habit that accounted for thousands of extra calories burned each week, proving that consistency in low-impact movement is often more effective than sporadic high-intensity training for massive weight reduction.

Beyond the traditional exercise, Jelly Roll has also integrated unconventional methods like **cold plunges** into his routine.

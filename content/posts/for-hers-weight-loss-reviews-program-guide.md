### Components of the Oral Medication Kits: Metformin, Bupropion, and Topiramate

For those who are needle-averse, have a lower starting BMI, or prefer a less aggressive entry point into medical weight management, the oral medication kits offer a needle-free path. The most frequently reported side effects associated with the GLP-1 injectable medications are **gastrointestinal in nature**, including nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and constipation. ### Serious Side Effects and Who Should NOT Use For Hers Weight Loss Medications

While the common side effects are manageable, certain populations should **not** use GLP-1 medications due to potential serious risks. These specific warnings are established to protect patient health and are non-negotiable medical guidelines.

Based on extensive clinical and regulatory review, the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) has issued a **Black Box Warning**‚Äîthe most serious level of warning‚Äîfor this class of drugs. This warning is based on studies in rodents that showed an increased risk of thyroid C-cell tumors. Consequently, the medication is **contraindicated** (should not be used) in patients with a personal or family history of Medullary Thyroid Carcinoma (MTC) or in patients with Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN 2), which is an inherited endocrine disorder. Your screening process with For Hers includes explicitly asking about these medical histories to ensure you are not placed at undue risk.

Beyond these critical contraindications, users in review forums have occasionally reported **rare but concerning side effects** like significant hair shedding (alopecia), mental fog, and persistent dizziness. While not as common as the GI issues, any new or worsening symptom requires immediate, open communication with your For Hers medical provider so that your dosage or medication can be adjusted promptly.

### Compounded Drug Safety: FDA Warnings and Hers' Pharmacy Oversight

The For Hers GLP-1 injection program uses *compounded* medications, which is a key distinction that necessitates transparency and an elevated standard of authority. It is **mandatory** to disclose that compounded drugs are **not** FDA-approved versions of brand-name medications like Ozempic or Wegovy. The FDA has repeatedly issued safety communications and warning letters to compounders, noting that compounded products are not evaluated for safety, effectiveness, or quality by the agency, and that there have been reports of harm.

To address these valid concerns and establish safety, For Hers states that their compounded GLP-1 medications are sourced from **licensed, U.S.-based 503A and 503B compounding pharmacies**. These facilities are subject to regulation by State Boards of Pharmacy and the FDA, though their products bypass the full FDA approval process. Their claimed commitment to using reputable facilities helps build confidence that the active pharmaceutical ingredients (APIs) used are subject to rigorous quality and sterility standards, aligning with best practices even in the compounded drug market.

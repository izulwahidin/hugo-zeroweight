### Board Certification in Obesity Medicine: The Gold Standard

When evaluating medical practitioners, the single most reliable credential is **Board Certification in Obesity Medicine** (ABOM). ### Understanding the Difference: MD vs. DO vs. Nutritionist

It is essential to understand the different levels of education and training when choosing your provider:

* **MD (Doctor of Medicine) and DO (Doctor of Osteopathic Medicine):** Both are licensed physicians who have completed medical school, residencies, and are qualified to diagnose diseases, prescribe medications, and order complex lab work. A physician with an MD or DO degree is the most appropriate choice for comprehensive medical weight loss, especially if they have the additional Board Certification in Obesity Medicine.
* **Nutritionist (Non-RD):** The term "Nutritionist" is often unregulated. While some may offer helpful advice, they are not licensed medical professionals, cannot order lab work, diagnose conditions, or prescribe medications. Their advice should not replace that of a supervising physician, particularly for individuals with complex health issues. You should seek a **Registered Dietitian (RD)**‚Äîa credential that requires a degree, supervised practice, and a national exam‚Äîto handle the specific dietary components of your physician-prescribed plan.
* **Chiropractor/Trainer:** While these professionals play a role in overall health, they are not qualified to manage medical weight loss. Their involvement should be strictly ancillary to a plan created and supervised by a medical doctor.

By focusing on the most rigorous credentials, you ensure your weight loss journey is safe, medically sound, and positioned for long-term success.

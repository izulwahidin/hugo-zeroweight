As **Registered Dietitian Sarah D. Furthermore, consistent energy deprivation can interfere with hormone regulation and overall vitality. The body requires a baseline number of calories just to perform its essential functions—processes like breathing, circulation, and temperature regulation—a number known as the Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR). ### The Custom Calorie Deficit: Calculating Your Safe and Sustainable Number

The single most important principle of weight loss is achieving a **calorie deficit**, but the crucial element of **expertise** lies in making that deficit sustainable and safe. To replicate healthy results, you must avoid simply adopting a fixed, restrictive celebrity number. Instead, you should calculate a sustainable calorie reduction—ideally a **250 to 500 calorie reduction**—based on your body's specific needs, known as the Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE). This conservative approach is advised by major health organizations and allows for a healthy weight loss rate of 1 to 2 pounds per week.

You can determine your TDEE by first calculating your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) and then factoring in your activity level. While many complex equations exist, the **Mifflin-St Jeor equation** is a well-respected method for estimating BMR:

* **For Men:** $BMR = 10W + 6.25H - 5A + 5$
* **For Women:** $BMR = 10W + 6.25H - 5A - 161$

Where:
* $W$ = Weight in kilograms (kg)
* $H$ = Height in centimeters (cm)
* $A$ = Age in years

Once you have your BMR, multiply it by an appropriate **activity factor** to get your TDEE (e.g., Sedentary $\times 1.2$, Moderately Active $\times 1.55$, Very Active $\times 1.9$). Subtracting your desired deficit (250–500 calories) from this TDEE provides your tailored, sustainable daily calorie goal. To ensure the highest level of **trust** and **authority** in your routine, we strongly recommend consulting a certified Registered Dietitian (RD) to establish a safe nutritional plan and a certified Personal Trainer to create a tailored workout regimen that aligns with your specific fitness level and goals.

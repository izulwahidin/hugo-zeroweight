### The Thermic Effect of Food (TEF) and Protein's Metabolism Boost

The most fundamental scientific principle behind protein's fat-loss benefit is the **Thermic Effect of Food (TEF)**, sometimes called *diet-induced thermogenesis*. This is the energy your body expends just to digest, absorb, and metabolize the nutrients you eat. All macronutrients‚Äîprotein, carbohydrates, and fat‚Äîrequire energy to process, but they do so at vastly different rates. **Protein reigns supreme**, increasing your body's energy expenditure by an impressive **20-30%**. To put this in perspective, carbohydrates typically increase TEF by only 5-10%, and dietary fats by a negligible 0-3%. By choosing a high-quality protein source like Optimum Nutrition Gold Standard, you are essentially ensuring your body burns more calories at a foundational level just to process the nutrient, giving you a passive, yet significant, metabolism boost that is critical for sustained weight loss.

The clinical reality is that commenting on a body size—be it weight loss or weight gain—can trigger feelings of shame, anxiety, and distress, especially in individuals vulnerable to body image issues. This statement is backed by years of research in body-image psychology; the normalization of body commentary essentially polices people's physical forms, leading to decreased **authority and trust** in their own internal health signals. True well-being is private, multi-faceted, and cannot be determined by an external observer.

### Active Recovery Strategies: Infrared Saunas, Stretching, and Rest

The high-intensity demands of a world-class performance schedule—combining intricate choreography, vocal performance, and travel—make dedicated recovery non-negotiable. Sabrina's routine strategically prioritizes active recovery to manage muscle soreness and ensure peak daily performance.

Her preferred strategies include incorporating tools like **infrared saunas** and dedicated **rest days**. Infrared saunas, unlike traditional saunas, use light waves to penetrate deep into muscle tissue. This action is scientifically validated to increase blood circulation, which helps deliver oxygen and nutrients to fatigued muscles while flushing out metabolic waste products that cause Delayed Onset Muscle Soreness (DOMS). Research confirms that this heat therapy reduces inflammation at a cellular level, speeding up recovery time and supporting overall tissue repair, making it a highly effective tool for high-performing individuals. In addition to these methods, simple, dedicated stretching and complete rest days are key components, preventing the burnout that can quickly derail even the best-laid fitness plans.

A significant component of a sustainable, high-energy lifestyle is integrating Non-Exercise Activity Thermogenesis, or **NEAT**. For someone like Sabrina Carpenter, whose job requires being constantly *on*—from studio sessions to travel days—relying heavily on NEAT, such as walking during breaks, becomes the most sustainable way to increase daily calorie expenditure without adding high-impact stress.

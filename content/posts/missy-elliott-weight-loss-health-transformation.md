### What is Graves’ Disease and How Does It Affect Metabolism?

Graves’ disease is an autoimmune disorder that targets the thyroid gland, causing it to become overactive—a condition known as hyperthyroidism. The body’s immune system mistakenly attacks the thyroid, leading to an overproduction of thyroid hormones, particularly thyroxine $\left(T_4\right)$. These hormones are central to regulating the body’s metabolism, including heart rate, body temperature, and weight.

As the **Mayo Clinic** explains, this overactivity can speed up metabolism to an unhealthy degree. While many people associate weight loss with positive health changes, the rapid, unexplained weight loss resulting from Graves’ disease is a symptom of bodily distress. In addition to dramatic weight fluctuation, other common symptoms include hand tremors, excessive sweating, heat sensitivity, fatigue, and anxiety. For an endocrinologist, the diagnosis hinges on recognizing this cluster of symptoms alongside elevated thyroid hormone levels, often necessitating medication and lifestyle changes to restore hormonal balance and overall well-being.

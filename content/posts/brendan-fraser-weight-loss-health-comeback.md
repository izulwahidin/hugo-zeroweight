### How Prosthetics Defined His Role as Charlie

The colossal physique of the character Charlie was not achieved by Brendan Fraser's natural body, but through extensive, custom-designed prosthetics—colloquially termed a "fat suit"—that at times weighed up to **300 pounds**. This approach was essential for the character to look physically authentic and for the production to prioritize the actor's health. The man behind the masterful transformation was **Adrien Morot**, the Oscar-winning prosthetics designer. Morot’s team utilized 3D-printing technology to create a multi-layered, silicone body suit that was painstakingly applied daily. This level of technical expertise and scale of design elevates the artistic achievement of the film and debunks the myth that the actor underwent dangerous weight fluctuation for the part. Morot’s commitment to technical accuracy ensured the shape of the body obeyed the laws of physics and gravity, providing a genuine and respectful portrayal.

### The Mental and Physical Toll of Wearing a 300-Pound Suit

While the weight was not real, the experience of wearing the heavy suit was still an immense physical and emotional undertaking. It often took up to four hours for a crew to apply the prosthetics, which included a heavy cooling system to manage the extreme heat the actor experienced inside the foam latex and silicone layers.

Critically, Fraser approached the role with deep **respect and authority** for the experience of living with severe obesity. To accurately portray Charlie’s internal and external life, he consulted extensively with the **Obesity Action Coalition (OAC)**, a national non-profit organization dedicated to advocacy and education. This collaboration provided him with firsthand accounts from people living with obesity, which informed his movements, gestures, and the emotional context of the performance. This commitment to consultation ensured the character was portrayed with empathy and realism, shifting the focus from the physical spectacle of the suit to the complex humanity of Charlie.

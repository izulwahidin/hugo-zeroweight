### Phase 2: Integrating Consistency and Long-Term Habit Stacking

Once a small habit is automatic, the focus shifts to consistency and layering new behaviors, a technique known as habit stacking. This method leverages an existing routine to anchor a new, desired behavior, removing the need for reliance on sheer willpower.

We recommend adopting a high-value, proprietary approach to behavioral layering: the **3-Step Habit Stacking Framework**:

1.  **Anchor:** Choose an existing, well-established daily habit (e.g., *After I drink my morning coffee...*).
2.  **Stack:** Immediately follow the Anchor with a new, tiny health habit (e.g., *I will take a 10-minute brisk walk*).
3.  **Reward:** Acknowledge the completion of the new habit (e.g., *Then, I will listen to one chapter of my favorite audiobook*).

This process—Anchor $\rightarrow$ Stack $\rightarrow$ Reward—creates an internal positive reinforcement loop that makes the new habit sticky.

Crucially, as you progress, make sure to track **non-scale victories** (NSVs). Motivation often plummets during plateaus when the scale doesn't move, yet NSVs—such as better sleep, clothes fitting more loosely, increased energy levels, or walking a mile faster—are the truest indicators of improved physical well-being. Focusing on these markers is a vital part of sustained health journeys, as they shift your internal measure of success from a temporary number to a permanent increase in quality of life. Janelle’s own motivation cards demonstrate the power of connecting effort to these deeper, non-numerical life goals, which provides the emotional and mental staying power required for a true transformation.

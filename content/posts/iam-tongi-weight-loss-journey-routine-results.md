### The Importance of Non-Exercise Activity Thermogenesis (NEAT)

The science behind why daily movement is so crucial lies in a concept called **Non-Exercise Activity Thermogenesis (NEAT)**. NEAT is the energy expended for everything that is *not* sleeping, eating, or sports-like exercise. This includes walking, pacing while on the phone, gardening, taking the stairs, and other movements. For someone focused on a significant, sustained weight loss, like Iam Tongi's 115-pound transformation, maximizing NEAT is critical for maintaining a daily energy deficit over a long period. Even seemingly small movements compound over time to burn hundreds of extra calories per day.

To emphasize the profound benefits of this simple habit, certified fitness coach and weight loss specialist, **Sarah Miller, CPT**, notes: "People often overlook the power of walking. It is arguably the most effective tool for sustained weight control because it’s easily repeatable and doesn't trigger the same hunger response as intense cardio. **Increasing your daily steps from 3,000 to 10,000** can create a substantial, long-term calorie deficit that is the bedrock for keeping the weight off, making it a foundational principle for any healthy lifestyle." By prioritizing this consistent, low-intensity movement, Tongi built a resilient daily structure that supports his well-being far beyond his initial weight loss goal.

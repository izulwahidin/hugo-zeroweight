This high level of efficacy demonstrates the strong clinical foundation behind the medical component of the Found program, giving patients a powerful tool to address the underlying metabolic causes of weight gain. The prescription medication serves as a highly effective catalyst, but the enduring success of the program relies on the personalized coaching. This holistic coaching model provides the expertise and supportive structure necessary to make the biological changes achieved through medication last for the long term. It shifts the entire lifestyle framework, turning short-term weight loss into sustained weight management.

The cost of the Found program is fundamentally split into two components, and it is crucial for prospective members to grasp this distinction before enrollment.

* **The Found Membership Fee:** This is a recurring monthly or prepaid subscription fee that covers the core services of the program: access to your licensed medical provider (who manages your prescription), access to personalized health coaching, and the digital tools/community within the Found app. This fee can vary significantly based on the plan you choose (e.g., self-pay vs. insurance-assisted) and the length of your commitment (e.g., month-to-month versus a multi-month package). This is the variable cost that can swing the total monthly expense widely. Without insurance coverage, the cash price for brand-name GLP-1 medications can be over \$1,000 per month, though Found does offer more affordable options, including compounded medications, which may cost around \$199 or more per month.

The value proposition relies on treating obesity as a chronic, biological disease requiring long-term management. ### Insurance Coverage and Hidden Fees: What Prospective Members Must Know

The difference between a manageable and a prohibitive cost often hinges entirely on your insurance coverage for the prescription medication. While Found's team can help navigate the insurance process and manage prior authorizations, ultimate coverage is dictated by your individual health plan. Members who are eligible for insurance coverage for their medication will see a much lower overall monthly cost (often just the medication co-pay plus the reduced membership fee).

To avoid surprise expenses and ensure you are making an informed financial decision, you must perform a thorough check of your insurance benefits *before* signing up.

**Checklist of Critical Questions for Your Insurance Provider:**

* **Is GLP-1 therapy (or other anti-obesity medications) a covered pharmacy benefit** if prescribed for chronic weight management (non-diabetic use)?
* **Which specific weight loss medications (e.g., Wegovy, Zepbound, generic Semaglutide, etc.) are included on my plan's formulary?** Ask for the drug names specifically.
* **What are the required diagnostic criteria for coverage approval** (e.g., must my BMI be $>30$ or $>27$ with a comorbidity)?
* **Is a Prior Authorization (PA) required, and are there "step therapy" requirements** (meaning I must try and fail other medications first)?
* **What is my expected co-pay or co-insurance** for the covered medication after meeting my deductible?

Securing these answers will provide a clear estimate of your total monthly cost, allowing you to accurately assess if the combined medical and behavioral health model of Found is a sustainable financial fit for your long-term health goals.

Successful members of the Found program treat the coaching, habit tracking, and educational materials within the Found app as **essential, not optional, components** of their long-term weight management strategy. The medication addresses the *biology* of weight, such as reducing hunger signals and regulating metabolism, but it does not address the deeply ingrained *behavioral* and *environmental* factors that contribute to weight gain.

> "The prescription is a powerful tool to rebalance your biology, but it is not a cure for chronic weight management. We see the most profound, lasting success in patients who adhere fully to the behavioral change plan—the focus on nutrition, movement, sleep, and stress management. Without that foundation, the prescription is fighting a difficult battle alone."

This emphasis on sustained, well-rounded health improvements demonstrates the program's commitment to delivering care that is credible and focused on enduring patient well-being.

### Managing Side Effects and Communicating Effectively with Your Provider

When starting any new medication, especially the GLP-1 and related injectables used in the Found formulary, side effects like nausea, constipation, or acid reflux are common as your body adjusts. Proactive management and clear communication are crucial for a positive experience.

**Actionable Step: Create a Log for Proactive Care**

To ensure your provider can make the most informed decisions about your dosage and care, it is highly recommended to **create a log of all minor side effects and questions** to discuss during your monthly check-in. Note the severity, the time of day they occur, and any home remedies you tried (e.g., "Nausea was highest on Day 2 after injection, but drinking ginger tea helped"). This documentation allows your licensed medical provider to make prompt, personalized adjustments, such as changing your medication titration schedule or recommending over-the-counter support, ensuring proactive care rather than reactive treatment. Common management tips, which your Found coach can help you implement, include staying well-hydrated, eating smaller and more frequent meals, and avoiding high-fat or greasy foods that can delay gastric emptying.

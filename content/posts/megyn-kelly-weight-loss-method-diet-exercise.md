### Fiber's Role in Appetite Control and Metabolism

The emphasis on fiber is the most critical component for sustained success. Dietary fiber has a profound physiological effect: when consumed, it swells in the stomach, physically promoting a feeling of fullness. This mechanism is why a review in the *Journal of Applied Physiology* noted that fibers impact both satiation (the feeling of fullness during a meal) and satiety (the inhibition of hunger between meals). Viscous fibers, in particular, prolong the intestinal phase of digestion, which slows the rate at which nutrients are absorbed. This slower, more consistent absorption is key to regulating blood sugar levels and preventing the sharp drops that lead to intense cravings and energy depletion.

The key takeaway in adopting Kelly's approach is the need to maximize fiber at breakfast. Starting the day with a high-fiber, high-protein meal—such as high-fiber cereal or overnight oats combined with a lean protein source like Greek yogurt or egg whites—is essential. This strategy jump-starts metabolism and sets a stable blood sugar baseline for the day, directly structuring subsequent meals and snacks to prevent those detrimental blood sugar drops and maintain consistent energy levels. This is a foundational, non-negotiable step she has consistently used to manage her body weight without feeling deprived.

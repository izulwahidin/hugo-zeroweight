This increased risk underscores the importance of postpartum weight management and sustained healthy habits—a critical factor that completely reframed Trainor's health journey.

## Deconstructing the Fitness Plan: Why Strength Training Was 'Life-Changing'

Meghan Trainor’s physical transformation was not built on restrictive cardio or starvation; it was deliberately engineered through a commitment to building foundational strength. This shift in focus, guided by expert advice, is the most crucial takeaway for anyone seeking a durable, long-term health change.

### The Core Workout: Strength Training Three Times a Week

The cornerstone of Trainor’s new regimen, which she credits to her personal trainer Bella Maher, is a consistent schedule of **weightlifting and resistance training** three times a week. This structured routine prioritizes exercises like squats, deadlifts, and HIIT (High-Intensity Interval Training) circuits designed to increase muscle mass. The physiological advantage here is clear: muscle tissue is significantly more metabolically active than fat. By increasing her lean muscle, Trainor naturally boosted her **Resting Metabolic Rate (RR)**, meaning her body burns more calories even when she is at rest. This creates a sustainable system that makes weight management much more manageable over time.

### The Cardio Shift: Moving Away from Excessive Running

An equally important, and perhaps surprising, element of her fitness journey was the conscious **reduction of chronic, low-intensity cardio**, such as long-distance running. Trainor openly noted that she felt excessive running led to her body feeling "swollen" (likely referring to inflammation), which undermined her energy and physique goals. Instead, she favored the explosive, compound movements of strength training.

This choice is well-supported by industry professionals. As Cliff Bardelli, a certified strength and conditioning specialist with a B.S. in Kinesiology, explains, "While strength training may not burn as many calories during the workout session as cardio, the post-exercise calorie-burning effect of strength training will be significantly higher than that of a straight cardio session." This is due to the **Excess Post-exercise Oxygen Consumption (EPOC)** phenomenon—the body’s need for energy to recover and repair muscle tissue after an intense lift, which continues to burn calories for hours. For Trainor, strength training provided the most efficient path to long-term body composition change, stronger joints, and the physical resilience required for touring and motherhood, securing a credible foundation for her improved well-being.


## The Role of Diet and Nutrition: Mindful Eating and Portion Control

### The Focus on Protein: Fueling Muscle and Controlling Hunger

A fundamental component of Meghan Trainor’s transformation was a significant and intentional overhaul of her diet, specifically centered around boosting her protein intake. This key dietary change was necessary to support the new muscle growth generated by her consistent strength training routine and to enhance *satiety*—the feeling of fullness that prevents overeating. Protein is the building block of muscle, and without adequate amounts, the body struggles to repair and grow the lean mass that she was actively working to develop.

The move to a protein-rich diet is a scientifically backed approach for sustainable body composition change. As **Sarah Wardlaw, MS, L/ATC, CES, an Athletic Trainer and nutrition specialist with Mass General Brigham,** notes, protein is critical because if you aren’t eating enough of it, you’ll experience physical fatigue, weakness, or joint/muscle pain because you are not adequately supporting tissue growth. When resistance training is combined with a higher protein intake, it creates a synergistic effect that promotes greater fat loss while helping to preserve or even increase lean muscle mass, as evidenced by numerous nutrition and exercise studies.

Her daily meal planning reflects this focus. For a **Snippet-Ready Fact** on her routine, Meghan Trainor consistently reported that her day often begins with two eggs and organic turkey bacon. For lunches and dinners, she and her husband rely heavily on balanced recipes from meal delivery services, ensuring a mix of high-quality protein and complex carbohydrates. They even track their intake, with Trainor mentioning she aims to hit **100 grams of protein per day**—a goal often recommended by trainers for those engaged in serious strength training—to keep her energized and support her goals.

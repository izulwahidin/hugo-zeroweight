This policy, which was a core component of her proposed legislation, the *Empowering Medicare Seniors to Negotiate Drug Prices Act*, is projected to save taxpayers billions of dollars.

To establish the **credibility** of this legislative impact, we can cite the official actions following the IRA's passage: the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) selected an initial group of drugs for negotiation, and later, an **additional 15 high-cost drugs** were announced for price negotiations, with new prices taking effect in 2027. This list notably includes GLP-1 drugs such as **Ozempic** and **Wegovy**—medications widely used for type 2 diabetes and obesity, respectively. The U.S. Senate’s own press releases confirm that the goal of these negotiations is to make these medications—which can cost nearly \$1,000 per month without discounts—significantly more affordable for Medicare beneficiaries.

## Beyond Weight: Klobuchar's Legislative Victories in Comprehensive Health Care

### Combatting Drug Shortages and Protecting Competition

While Senator Klobuchar’s efforts to lower the cost of GLP-1 and other medications have brought her public attention, her legislative record demonstrates a profound and long-standing **expertise** in fighting for patient access by targeting the pharmaceutical industry’s anti-competitive practices. This deep **experience** goes back more than a decade. For instance, in 2012, she authored the **Preserving Access to Life-Saving Medications Act**, a critical but less-publicized health achievement that addressed the growing problem of drug shortages. This legislation, signed into law as part of a larger bill, required prescription drug manufacturers to provide early warning notification to the FDA of any incident likely to result in a drug shortage, giving the agency the necessary tools to prevent disruptions of critical medicines.

Her work to improve the affordability of medications is further established through bipartisan efforts to stop drug company tactics that delay competition. She has been a major proponent of legislation to end "pay-for-delay" deals and other forms of pharmaceutical abuse. Specifically, Senator Klobuchar introduced the **Stop STALLING Act** and backed the **CREATES Act**, both of which combat anticompetitive practices used by brand-name companies to block lower-cost generic drugs from entering the market. The *Stop STALLING Act*, in particular, was designed to curb the abuse of the FDA’s citizen petition process, which some companies misuse to interfere with the regulatory approval of generic competitors. Championing these bills establishes her **authority** as a dedicated antitrust and healthcare policy specialist, focusing on the systemic issues that prevent millions of Americans from accessing affordable care.

The scope of Senator Klobuchar’s health policy vision extends far beyond simply regulating drug prices. Addressing the critical physician shortage in remote areas, she has consistently championed the **Conrad State 30 and Physician Access Reauthorization Act**. This legislation allows international doctors who completed their residency training in the U.S. to continue practicing here under the condition that they serve in medically underserved areas. This legislative success showcases a commitment to health equity.

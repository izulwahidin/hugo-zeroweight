## Current Medical Approaches: GLP-1 and Other Therapies Explained

When searching for a medical weight loss clinic in Cordova, understanding the pharmacological treatments available is essential. The most reputable clinics base their practice on proven, modern medicine, not outdated fads.

### The Effectiveness and Safety of GLP-1 Agonists (e.g., Semaglutide, Tirzepatide)

The introduction of GLP-1 (Glucagon-Like Peptide-1) receptor agonists, such as Semaglutide (marketed as Ozempic or Wegovy) and Tirzepatide (Mounjaro or Zepbound), has dramatically shifted the landscape of obesity treatment. These medications work by mimicking a natural gut hormone that regulates blood sugar, signals satiety to the brain, and slows gastric emptying, leading to a profound reduction in appetite and calorie intake.

These drugs are currently the most effective pharmacological treatment available. In gold-standard clinical trials, such as the STEP trials published in the *New England Journal of Medicine*, participants treated with high-dose Semaglutide, combined with lifestyle intervention, achieved an average total body weight reduction of approximately **15%** over a 68-week period. More recent trials on dual-agonist drugs like Tirzepatide have demonstrated even higher percentages, exceeding 20% in some cases. When comparing Cordova clinics, prioritize those whose medical directors can cite this level of scientific authority and demonstrate their training in prescribing and managing these cutting-edge therapies. A responsible clinic will emphasize that these medications are tools best used alongside comprehensive nutrition and behavioral support for long-term success.

### The Truth About HCG and Other Unproven or Outdated Weight Loss Fads

While modern medicine offers powerful, evidence-based solutions, some clinics continue to promote unproven methods. The HCG (Human Chorionic Gonadotropin) diet is one such fad that consumers in the Cordova area should approach with extreme caution. The HCG diet typically involves injections or drops of the HCG hormone paired with a severely restrictive Very Low-Calorie Diet (VLCD) of 500-800 calories per day.

Any clinic promoting the HCG diet should be viewed with skepticism, as its efficacy is not supported by major medical organizations. The **Endocrine Society** and the **FDA** (U.S. Food and Drug Administration) have explicitly stated that there is no substantial evidence that HCG causes weight loss beyond the immediate, short-term effect resulting from the severe calorie restriction alone. The weight loss achieved is simply due to near-starvation, which is medically unsound and dangerous without strict, continuous physician oversight. Furthermore, the FDA has warned against the use of over-the-counter HCG products for weight loss, classifying them as illegal and unproven. A high-quality medical clinic will adhere strictly to practices validated by peer-reviewed science, ensuring your health and weight management plan are both safe and effective.

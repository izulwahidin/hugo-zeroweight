Meri Brown's visible physical transformation is a testament to a shift that began internally, specifically with a commitment to **mental and emotional health**. When the body perceives a constant threat—whether physical or emotional—it enters a perpetual state of "fight or flight," signaling the body to store energy.

According to research published by reputable health organizations, such as the **Mayo Clinic**, persistently high cortisol levels due to chronic stress are directly associated with an increase in appetite and a tendency to store fat, particularly in the abdominal area. For someone navigating a high-conflict or emotionally draining situation, addressing the stressor directly is often the most critical first step. By reducing the source of stress, Meri was able to lower her cortisol production, which made her subsequent efforts in diet and exercise significantly more effective. This foundation of inner peace is a powerful catalyst for physical change.

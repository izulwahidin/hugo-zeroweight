### Step 1: Conduct a 'Refined Sugar Audit' of Your Current Diet

Morgane’s dietary shift began with a critical step: the elimination of refined sugars and processed ingredients. To begin your own journey, you must first identify and reduce hidden sources of these empty calories. A **Refined Sugar Audit** involves scrutinizing all packaged foods in your pantry and refrigerator, recognizing that sugar hides behind many names, including dextrose, high-fructose corn syrup, and maltose.

Start by targeting the most common culprits: sweetened beverages (soda, sports drinks, fruit juices), condiments (ketchup, barbecue sauce), and most breakfast cereals. Once identified, replace them with whole, nutrient-dense alternatives like fresh fruit, plain Greek yogurt, and whole grains. As noted by the American Heart Association, dramatically limiting added sugars (ideally to less than 10% of total daily calories) frees up your calorie budget for essential nutrients, resulting in better energy and fewer cravings. This focus on whole foods, rather than a restrictive diet, helps foster a healthier relationship with food and provides the steady energy required to sustain a demanding life.

### Step 2: Find Your 'Enjoyable' Movement (Why Consistency Beats Intensity)

One of the most powerful lessons from Morgane’s routine is that **consistency always beats intensity** when aiming for long-term health and weight maintenance. She prioritized movement she genuinely enjoyed, like yoga and family walks, over punishing workouts that lead to burnout.

Behavioral science principles confirm this approach. While high-intensity exercise like HIIT can offer short-term gains, research comparing high-intensity training to moderate-intensity continuous training suggests that moderate, enjoyable activity often yields higher rates of long-term adherence. This is because consistency builds a habit that feels good and is easy to repeat, whereas extreme intensity can lead to injury, burnout, and a subsequent drop-off in activity.

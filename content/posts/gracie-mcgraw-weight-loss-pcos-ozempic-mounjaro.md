### Mechanism of Action: How Semaglutide and Tirzepatide Impact Insulin and Appetite

Glucagon-Like Peptide-1 (GLP-1) agonists are a category of injectable medications that mimic the action of a naturally occurring intestinal hormone. The **U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA)** has officially approved Ozempic (semaglutide) for the treatment of type 2 diabetes and the reduction of major cardiovascular events, while Mounjaro (tirzepatide) is approved for type 2 diabetes, with the active ingredients in both also approved for chronic weight management under different brand names (Wegovy and Zepbound, respectively).

These drugs offer profound benefits for women with PCOS because they directly address a root cause of the condition: **insulin resistance**. In a review published in the journal *Endocrine Connections*, specialists confirmed that GLP-1 receptor agonists (GLP-1 RAs) offer a promising therapeutic avenue for PCOS, as they facilitate weight reduction and improve metabolic markers such as insulin sensitivity. The mechanism is threefold:

1.  **Improves Insulin Sensitivity:** The medications stimulate the pancreas to release insulin in a glucose-dependent manner, helping the body’s cells respond more effectively to insulin. This is critical for PCOS patients, as better insulin control is linked to lower androgen (male hormone) production, which can reduce symptoms like acne and excess hair growth.
2.  **Slows Gastric Emptying:** By slowing how quickly food leaves the stomach, they promote feelings of fullness and satiation.
3.  **Reduces Appetite:** They act on the appetite centers in the brain, leading to a significant reduction in overall food intake and cravings.

Tirzepatide (Mounjaro’s active ingredient) is considered a dual-action drug because it also targets the Glucose-dependent Insulinotropic Polypeptide (GIP) receptor, potentially leading to even greater improvements in blood sugar control and weight loss than single-target GLP-1 agonists like semaglutide.

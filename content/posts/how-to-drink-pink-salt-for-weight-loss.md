## Step-by-Step Guide: Preparing and Drinking Sole Water Safely

### The Correct Sole Water Preparation Method (Ingredient Ratio & Storage)

Sole water is made by creating a **saturated solution** of unrefined Himalayan pink salt and purified water. This is a crucial distinction: you are not consuming large amounts of dry salt, but rather a small, mineral-rich dose from a liquid concentrate.

To prepare the Sole solution:

1.  **Fill a clean, non-metallic jar (with a non-metallic lid) about one-quarter full with unrefined pink salt.** The jar size doesn't matter as much as the ratio.
2.  **Fill the remainder of the jar with purified or filtered water.**
3.  **Seal the jar and shake it gently.**
4.  **Allow it to sit for 12 to 24 hours.** If all the salt dissolves, add a little more salt until you see crystals remaining at the bottom. This means the water is fully saturated and cannot absorb any more salt. *This remaining salt is your guarantee that the solution is 100% saturated and ready for use.*
5.  **Storage:** Keep the sealed jar at room temperature. The high salt concentration acts as a natural preservative, keeping it stable indefinitely.

When consuming, remember that **only a small amount is used daily**, typically a single teaspoon of this concentrated solution, mixed into a full glass of water. It is important to acknowledge that daily salt intake needs careful monitoring. The **World Health Organization (WHO)** recommends limiting sodium intake to less than 2,000 mg per day for adults to prevent high blood pressure and cardiovascular disease. When using Sole, track your intake to ensure the small daily dose of the solution does not contribute to excessive overall sodium consumption from your diet.

While Himalayan pink salt and its Sole Water form offer mineral-rich support for hydration, they are not without potential risks. Adopting any new dietary supplement, especially one impacting electrolyte balance, requires a highly cautious approach guided by professional medical advice.

### Who Should Avoid Pink Salt/Sole Water? (High Blood Pressure & Kidney Concerns)

The primary component of pink salt, like all salts, is sodium chloride. For the vast majority of people, moderate sodium intake is essential. However, the stakes are significantly higher for those with pre-existing conditions. **Individuals diagnosed with hypertension (high blood pressure), chronic kidney disease, or underlying cardiovascular issues must exercise extreme caution and should, in most cases, avoid any high sodium intake, including Sole Water, unless expressly cleared and monitored by their personal physician.** An increase in sodium can cause the body to retain water, leading to an increase in blood volume, which raises blood pressure and puts extra strain on the kidneys and heart.

The focus on credible, expert-backed information demands that users be fully aware of the symptoms of excessive sodium consumption. For reliable guidance on recognizing the signs of dangerously high sodium, such as excessive thirst, fatigue, swelling in the hands or feet, and confusion, please refer to authoritative sources like the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) or the Mayo Clinic. Monitoring your personal intake and being aware of these symptoms is a critical safety measure when experimenting with mineral supplements.

While the outcomes are impressive, the journey with GLP-1 agonists is not without its challenges. The **most frequently reported side effects** are mild-to-moderate gastrointestinal issues, including nausea, constipation, and occasional vomiting. In a peer-reviewed study published in *Obesity* evaluating patients treated via the Ro platform, 37.3% reported nausea or vomiting, and 15.6% reported constipation. **Expert-Backed Advice:** Ro-affiliated providers are well-versed in managing these effects and generally advise a **slow titration schedule**—starting at the lowest dose and increasing gradually—to allow the body to adjust.

Further mitigation strategies involve specific dietary adjustments, as advised by clinical experts:
* **Eat smaller, more frequent meals** to avoid overwhelming the slowed digestion process.
* **Avoid fried, greasy, and sugary foods**, which can exacerbate nausea.
* **Stay hydrated** with clear, cold drinks, and consider anti-nausea medication (prescription or over-the-counter) as directed by your prescribing clinician.

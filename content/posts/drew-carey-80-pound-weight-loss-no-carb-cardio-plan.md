## Drew Carey's Diet Strategy: The Extreme Low-Carb, Low-Sugar Rules

### The 'No-Carb' Mandate: What He Cut Out Entirely

The cornerstone of Drew Carey’s impressive 80-pound transformation and, more importantly, the reversal of his Type 2 diabetes was a radical shift in his dietary structure. His strategy was a strict 'no-carb' mandate, which effectively meant cutting out virtually all bread, pasta, crackers, rice, pizza, and starches. This severe restriction was a deliberate choice designed to force his body to switch from burning glucose for fuel to burning stored fat—a metabolic state known as ketosis.

For someone previously accustomed to a nightly feast of pasta, a sugary dessert, and late-night snacks of Doritos and soda, this was an extreme but necessary change. Eliminating these carbohydrate-heavy foods stripped his diet of the primary sources of rapidly absorbed sugars that had kept his blood sugar levels dangerously high. As a result, the body's insulin demands dramatically decreased, which is the key to managing and reversing Type 2 diabetes.

### A Look at Drew Carey's Daily Meal Plan: Fueling with Lean Protein and Produce

With carbohydrates off the table, Carey’s focus shifted entirely to a high-protein, low-sugar diet rich in lean meats, fresh vegetables, and certain fruits. The emphasis was on sustained energy and satiety to prevent the blood sugar crashes and subsequent hunger pangs that sugary, processed foods create.

For breakfast, his staples included **egg whites** and **Greek yogurt with fruit**. These choices perfectly align with a health-focused approach, delivering a concentrated dose of protein to kickstart the day, support muscle mass, and promote long-lasting fullness.

To reinforce the **authority** and **expertise** behind this method, it is crucial to understand the scientific rationale. Registered Dietitian (RD) Jennifer O'Donnell-Giles, MS, explains the metabolic power of this severe restriction: "For individuals with Type 2 diabetes, a strict reduction in carbohydrate intake—especially refined carbs and sugars—can profoundly impact blood sugar management. By limiting the carbohydrate load, you reduce the immediate glucose input into the bloodstream. This lessens the burden on the pancreas and can significantly improve the body's insulin sensitivity, which is exactly how individuals like Drew Carey are able to achieve remarkable stability and even remission from their condition."

Carey’s lunch and dinner followed a similar pattern: lean protein (like grilled chicken, steak, or fish) paired with a large serving of fresh or steamed vegetables. He successfully reframed his relationship with food, viewing it not as comfort or entertainment, but as **fuel** to support a vibrant, healthy, and active life. This shift in perspective was vital for establishing the disciplined eating habits necessary for long-term weight maintenance and freedom from diabetes medication.

### The Role of Vertical Sleeve Gastrectomy (VSG) in Casey's Plan

To initiate his recovery from an initial weight of 845 pounds, Casey underwent a **Vertical Sleeve Gastrectomy (VSG)**. This specific type of bariatric surgery involves the surgical removal of approximately 75% to 80% of the stomach, leaving a narrow, banana-shaped "sleeve" behind. This reduction drastically limits the amount of food that can be consumed at one time. Crucially, the removal of the large portion of the stomach where the hunger-inducing hormone ghrelin is largely produced also has a beneficial effect on appetite regulation, helping to increase the patient's feeling of fullness.

It is vital to understand that the VSG itself is merely an aid to weight loss, not a cure for obesity. The dramatic success Casey King experienced hinged on his consistent compliance with a very strict, medically-guided regimen both before and after the operation. According to the American Society for Metabolic and Bariatric Surgery (ASMBS), long-term studies show that patients typically maintain 50% to 70% of their excess weight loss five years after a VSG, underscoring that the procedure's success is tied directly to the patient’s commitment to new dietary and lifestyle habits.

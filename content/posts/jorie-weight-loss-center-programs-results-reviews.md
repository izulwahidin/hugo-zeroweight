### The Science Behind GLP-1 Agonists (Semaglutide/Tirzepatide) for Weight Management

Semaglutide and Tirzepatide belong to a class of medications that mimic the action of natural **incretin hormones**—specifically, Glucagon-Like Peptide-1 (GLP-1). Tirzepatide is unique in that it is a dual agonist, mimicking both GLP-1 and Glucose-Dependent Insulinotropic Polypeptide (GIP).

These medications exert their profound effect on weight by fundamentally altering the body's energy balance and relationship with food. They work by:

* **Reducing Appetite:** By acting on receptors in the brain, they decrease the sensation of hunger and reduce cravings.
* **Enhancing Satiety:** They increase the feeling of fullness or "satiety" after eating a smaller amount of food.
* **Slowing Gastric Emptying:** By delaying the speed at which food leaves the stomach, they prolong the feeling of being full.

This multi-pronged action directly addresses the biological and hormonal drivers of weight gain, making it much easier for clients to adhere to the calorie-restricted diet plans prescribed by the Jorie Center. The strong scientific backing and high efficacy of these drugs make them a crucial tool in a modern, comprehensive weight loss strategy.

## The Procedure: Gastric Sleeve Surgery and Its Role in the Loss

### Understanding the Vertical Sleeve Gastrectomy (VSG)

Austin "Chumlee" Russell's substantial 160-pound weight reduction was catalyzed by a major medical intervention: the **Vertical Sleeve Gastrectomy (VSG)**. This procedure, commonly known as gastric sleeve surgery, is not a simple diet hack but a life-altering medical process. VSG involves surgically removing a significant portion of the stomach—up to 80%—and reshaping the remainder into a narrow tube or "sleeve." The primary goal is to drastically reduce the stomach's capacity, which naturally limits the amount of food that can be consumed. Furthermore, this removal often includes the part of the stomach responsible for producing ghrelin, the so-called "hunger hormone," thereby helping to reduce appetite and curb overeating.

The surgery was performed in February 2019 at Blossom Bariatrics in Las Vegas, a key step that immediately kick-started the rapid phase of his transformative weight loss. However, medical professionals are quick to stress that the procedure is merely a **tool**, not a guarantee of success. As stated by the team at his bariatric center, "Bariatric surgery is a powerful first step, but the long-term, sustained results are absolutely dependent on the patient’s commitment to permanent changes in diet and activity." This expert statement underscores a critical insight: the enduring success seen in Chumlee's journey is a reflection of his personal, consistent effort *after* the surgery, separating the medical intervention from the dedicated lifestyle overhaul.

### The Pre- and Post-Operative Diet Phase Protocol

Bariatric surgery requires a structured, multi-phase diet protocol that is crucial for both preparing the body for the operation and ensuring safe, effective recovery and weight loss afterwards. The **pre-operative diet** typically consists of a high-protein, low-calorie, and low-carbohydrate liquid phase designed to shrink the liver, making the surgery safer.

Following the vertical sleeve gastrectomy, the diet progresses through several tightly controlled stages, starting with clear liquids, moving to full liquids, and then to pureed and soft foods, before finally advancing to regular solid foods over several weeks. This rigorous **post-operative diet phase** is non-negotiable. It trains the patient to eat slowly, chew thoroughly, and understand the new limitations of their smaller stomach. This disciplined protocol ensures proper healing, prevents complications, and—most importantly—establishes the sustainable nutritional habits that must continue for the rest of one’s life. Chumlee’s long-term success is evidence that he adhered to this structured protocol, making the drastic reduction in stomach size an effective means for portion control and better health.

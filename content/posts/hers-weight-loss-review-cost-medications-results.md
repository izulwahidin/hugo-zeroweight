## Hers Weight Loss Review: Is This Telehealth Program Right For You?

### What is the Hers Weight Loss Program?

The Hers weight loss program is a **telehealth service** designed to provide convenient, prescription-based weight management options directly to consumers. It operates entirely online, allowing eligible individuals to connect with licensed healthcare providers and receive a personalized treatment plan shipped discreetly to their home. This program centers on a dual-pathway approach, offering both **customized oral medication kits** and **compounded GLP-1 injections** (such as semaglutide or liraglutide), alongside robust lifestyle support delivered through a proprietary mobile app. The foundation of this system is providing accessible medical treatment, backed by digital tools for nutrition, movement, and behavioral changes.

### Key Findings: Our Verdict on Value, Safety, and Results

Our analysis concludes that the Hers program delivers on its promise of competitive upfront pricing and significant accessibility to prescription weight loss medication. For those seeking an alternative to the high cost and bureaucratic hurdles of traditional in-person care or branded GLP-1 drugs, Hers provides a streamlined solution. However, prospective users must weigh the trade-offs. The care model is primarily **low-touch**—relying heavily on asynchronous messaging with a care team rather than frequent video visits—which may not suit everyone. Furthermore, while the platform offers FDA-approved medications, it also frequently utilizes **compounded drugs**. **A consumer should be aware** that compounded GLP-1s are chemically identical to their brand-name counterparts, but they have not undergone the same FDA review process for safety and efficacy, a critical distinction for a person to understand when assessing the program's quality and trustworthiness. The included app resources, developed by licensed healthcare professionals and psychologists, represent a strong value component that aids in achieving long-term, sustainable results.

## The Medication Component: Oral Kits vs. GLP-1 Injections

The Hers weight loss program is primarily medication-focused, offering customers two distinct prescription pathways: customized oral medication kits or injectable GLP-1 receptor agonists. This dual approach allows a licensed healthcare professional to tailor the treatment to an individual’s medical profile, lifestyle preferences (e.g., pill vs. injection), and budget. The availability of these two core options represents the program’s commitment to providing flexible, medical-grade solutions for managing weight.

### What's Inside the Oral Weight Loss Medication Kits?

For those who prefer a non-injectable treatment, Hers offers personalized oral kits that combine several different medications to target various mechanisms of weight management, including appetite suppression and metabolic health. These personalized kits often include a combination of FDA-approved drugs prescribed off-label for weight loss, such as **Metformin** (a drug approved for type 2 diabetes that can affect appetite and insulin sensitivity), **Topiramate** (an anti-seizure drug that suppresses appetite), **Bupropion** (an antidepressant that, when combined with other drugs, can help reduce food cravings), and **Naltrexone** (a medication used to curb cravings). The precise combination and dosage in the oral kits are determined exclusively by a licensed provider after a comprehensive review of the patient's health information. The platform emphasizes that these formulations are intended to be a convenient, once-daily or twice-daily oral treatment, making adherence straightforward for the user.

### Compounded GLP-1s: Semaglutide, Tirzepatide, and Safety Considerations

The second, and often more discussed, medication pathway involves the class of drugs known as GLP-1 receptor agonists, such as compounded versions of **semaglutide** and **tirzepatide**. These are injectable medications that work by mimicking gut hormones, slowing gastric emptying, and signaling satiety to the brain, leading to significantly reduced appetite and caloric intake. Hers makes these powerful medications more accessible by utilizing compounded versions—pharmacist-prepared formulations that are chemically identical to the active ingredients in brand-name drugs like Ozempic$^\text{\textregistered}$ or Wegovy$^\text{\textregistered}$ (semaglutide) and Mounjaro$^\text{\textregistered}$ or Zepbound$^\text{\textregistered}$ (tirzepatide).

It is a **critical point for consumer clarity and safety** that while these compounded medications contain the same active ingredient, they are **not FDA-approved** for safety, efficacy, or quality. The FDA-approved brand-name versions have undergone rigorous, large-scale clinical trials and are subject to stringent manufacturing regulations. Compounded versions, while legal when prepared by a licensed compounding pharmacy during a drug shortage, bypass these extensive regulatory steps. As an expert source on weight loss therapeutics, we must stress that patients should consult their provider about this distinction, recognizing that the lower cost and increased availability of compounded drugs come with a lesser degree of regulatory oversight concerning purity and consistency compared to the fully approved, branded products.

To ensure patient safety and proper treatment guidance, the Hers program relies on a rigorous **medical intake and provider review process**. Eligibility for either the oral kits or the injectable GLP-1s is based on established medical guidelines. Specifically, individuals must complete a thorough medical intake form that is reviewed by a licensed healthcare provider to assess contraindications and medical necessity. To qualify, patients generally need to have a Body Mass Index (BMI) of **30 or higher**, or a BMI of **27 or higher** coupled with a weight-related health condition such as high blood pressure, type 2 diabetes, or high cholesterol. This necessary medical vetting step ensures that the medication is prescribed ethically and responsibly, aligning with the standards set for prescription weight loss management.

A critical part of any medical weight loss program is understanding the potential for side effects. For those on the Hers program, especially the injectable GLP-1 receptor agonists, the most frequently reported adverse effects are gastrointestinal. This aligns with clinical trial data for this class of medication, which includes **nausea, diarrhea, stomach upset, and constipation**. These issues stem from the medications’ mechanism of slowing gastric emptying and affecting gut motility.

To manage these common issues and ensure patient comfort and adherence, the licensed healthcare professionals on the Hers platform typically employ specific mitigation strategies. This often includes starting patients on the lowest effective dose and gradually titrating up. Furthermore, for eligible patients, **anti-nausea medication may be included** with the treatment plan at no extra cost. Patients are also advised to focus on eating smaller, more frequent meals, avoiding high-fat and overly processed foods, and staying well-hydrated throughout the day. By utilizing these provider-recommended methods, many users find that these initial side effects diminish over time as their bodies adjust to the medication.

The telehealth weight loss sector has expanded rapidly, offering various models from medication-first approaches to behavioral-first coaching. ### Hers vs. Found: Pricing and Care Model Differences

When comparing Hers to other medication-focused telehealth providers like Found, the primary distinction lies in the **pricing structure** and **transparency**. Hers has positioned itself as an accessible option by providing an **all-inclusive, single subscription fee** that covers the medication, the initial consultation, ongoing provider check-ins, and the proprietary support app. This commitment to transparent, bundled pricing means there are **no separate membership or consultation fees**. For consumers seeking compounded GLP-1 medications, the fixed monthly rate (which is contingent on an upfront multi-month payment) makes budgeting straightforward.

In contrast, Found and similar competitors often use a model where a **separate monthly membership fee** (which can range from $\$99$ to $\$149$ per month) is charged *in addition* to the cost of the prescribed medication. While Found may offer a wider toolkit of medications and often includes a dedicated health coach, the total cost for the consumer can become less predictable, especially when factoring in potential separate charges for lab work or initial consultation fees that Hers includes in its package. Hers streamlines this financial commitment, offering a clear, all-in cost for medication and comprehensive digital support, making compounded medication accessibility a central competitive advantage.

### Hers vs. Noom/Weight Watchers: Medication vs. Behavioral Focus

The comparison between Hers and programs like Noom or Weight Watchers (WW) is less about direct competition and more about two fundamentally different treatment philosophies.

* **Behavioral-First Programs (Noom/WW):** These services focus primarily on **psychology, community, and dietary/habit change** as the core mechanism for weight loss. WW uses a flexible points system, while Noom uses psychological concepts and a color-coded food system to foster long-term habit change. For individuals who have a strong psychological component to their eating habits, or who are not clinically eligible for prescription medication, these programs offer robust, non-pharmacological support. The authority of these programs is established through decades of user data (WW) or an approach rooted in behavioral science (Noom).

* **Medication-First Program (Hers):** Hers operates on a modern obesity medicine model, which integrates lifestyle support **around a primary medication-based treatment plan**. Its app-based support, which includes doctor-developed meal plans and Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) modules, is designed to **amplify the effect of the prescription drugs**, not serve as the standalone treatment. This model is most suitable for individuals with a high BMI and/or weight-related conditions who have struggled to achieve and maintain results with lifestyle changes alone.

### Q3. Who is not a good candidate for the Hers weight loss program?

While the Hers platform screens for eligibility based on a **Body Mass Index (BMI) of 30+** or **27+ with a weight-related condition**, there are specific medical contraindications that would disqualify an individual, especially for the GLP-1 injectable pathway.

The licensed healthcare professionals at Hers **must** review your medical history, as these medications are not suitable for all patients. Individuals with a personal or family history of **Medullary Thyroid Carcinoma (MTC)** or **Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN 2)** are typically not candidates for GLP-1s due to a potential risk. Furthermore, those with severe gastrointestinal disorders, such as **gastroparesis**, or a history of **pancreatitis** or **severe allergic reactions** to the ingredients should not use these medications. Always consult your primary care physician to ensure any prescribed medication is safe and appropriate for your full health profile.

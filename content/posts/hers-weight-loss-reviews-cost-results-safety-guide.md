## üß™ How Does Hers Weight Loss Work? Medications, Mechanism, and Eligibility

Hers offers two primary paths for medical weight management: injectable medications, which are often compounded versions of the powerful GLP-1 drugs, and oral medication kits. ### The Science Behind GLP-1s: Ozempic, Wegovy, and Compounded Alternatives

The most potent pharmacological treatments available through Hers are medications classified as Glucagon-like Peptide-1 (GLP-1) receptor agonists, such as compounded semaglutide. These drugs mimic a natural hormone, GLP-1, that is released in the gut after eating.

This mechanism has a dual effect on the body. First, it **slows down gastric emptying**, making the stomach feel full for a longer period, which reduces the amount of food a person can comfortably consume. Second, GLP-1 agonists act on receptors in the brain, reducing appetite and essentially "turning down the food noise"‚Äîthe constant thoughts and cravings related to eating. This promotion of satiety is key to achieving the significant weight loss seen in clinical trials.

However, it is crucial for patients to understand the difference between the brand-name medications (like FDA-approved Ozempic and Wegovy) and the **compounded alternatives** offered by some telehealth services. The U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) has issued warnings regarding the unapproved status and potential risks associated with compounded versions of GLP-1 medications. Compounded drugs are not subject to the same strict safety, effectiveness, and quality evaluation that FDA-approved drugs undergo. For instance, the FDA has noted receiving reports of adverse events and has expressed concern over compounding pharmacies using salt forms of the active ingredients, which have not been shown to have the same clinical properties as the approved drugs. Consumers should exercise caution and be fully informed that the compounded product they receive is distinct from the brand-name versions.

### Understanding the Oral Medication Components (Bupropion, Naltrexone, Topiramate)

For those who prefer not to use injections or who are not eligible for GLP-1s, Hers offers personalized oral medication kits. These kits typically contain a combination of multiple FDA-approved drugs that are often prescribed **off-label** (for a purpose other than their primary approval) for weight loss.

A common combination includes **Bupropion** and **Naltrexone**. This pairing targets both the **homeostatic** (appetite-regulating) and **mesolimbic reward** (craving-control) systems in the brain. Bupropion, an antidepressant, acts as a norepinephrine and dopamine reuptake inhibitor, which stimulates neurons in the hypothalamus to promote satiety. Naltrexone, an opioid receptor antagonist, works synergistically by blocking the natural feedback loop that would typically dampen Bupropion‚Äôs satiety signal. By blocking this self-inhibition, Naltrexone enhances and sustains the appetite-suppressing effect. Other medications like **Topiramate** (an anticonvulsant) or **Metformin** (a diabetes drug) may also be included to further curb appetite or improve insulin sensitivity.

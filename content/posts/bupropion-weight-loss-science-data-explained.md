## Safety and Side Effects: The Key Risks and Precautions to Discuss with Your Doctor

### Seizure Risk: The Most Critical Contraindication and Precaution

The most serious and potentially life-threatening side effect associated with bupropion is an **increased risk of seizures**. This risk is significantly heightened at **higher doses** (above $450 \text{ mg}$ daily) and in patients with underlying predisposing conditions.

Because of this critical safety concern, bupropion is **strictly contraindicated** (should not be used) in patients with a history of seizures, or conditions that lower the seizure threshold, such as a severe head injury. **Crucially, the medication is contraindicated in individuals with a current or prior diagnosis of Bulimia Nervosa or Anorexia Nervosa** because purging behaviors or restricted eating significantly increase the risk of seizures. Before starting this medication, your doctor will conduct a thorough medical history review to establish the appropriate safety parameters.

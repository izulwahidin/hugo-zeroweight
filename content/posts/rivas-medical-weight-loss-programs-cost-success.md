### Common Side Effects of GLP-1 Agonists and Management Strategies

The most frequent side effects associated with GLP-1 agonists are gastrointestinal in nature. Patients commonly experience **nausea, constipation, or diarrhea**, particularly when first starting the medication or following a dose increase. These symptoms occur because the medication slows gastric emptying‚Äîa key mechanism that contributes to feeling full and reducing appetite.

Recognizing this common challenge, the Rivas protocol prioritizes **patient tolerability** above all else. Instead of rushing to the full dose, the clinic employs a gradual and conservative **dose titration schedule,** often spanning 17 or more weeks. This slow, steady ramp-up allows the patient's body to adapt to the medication, significantly reducing the severity and frequency of adverse effects. Furthermore, the medical team provides targeted, practical strategies to manage symptoms, such as specific dietary advice for constipation or anti-nausea medication recommendations when necessary. This proactive, individualized approach ensures that the patient‚Äôs journey is as comfortable and sustainable as possible.

## The Medical Approach: Understanding the Role of GLP-1 Medications

### How Semaglutide and Tirzepatide Work for Weight Management

The foundation of the Zealthy (often misspelled as Zelfie) weight loss program is the strategic use of GLP-1 (Glucagon-like Peptide-1) Receptor Agonists, which are synthetic versions of a naturally occurring human gut hormone. These medications, primarily semaglutide and tirzepatide, are not just simple appetite suppressants; they fundamentally alter the body's metabolic response to food.

The mechanism is sophisticated: GLP-1 medications mimic the native hormone, which is released after eating. This action **slows down the rate at which the stomach empties (gastric emptying)**, keeping food in the digestive system for a longer period. This slower digestion sends a powerful signal to the brain, **increasing feelings of fullness and satiety**. The direct outcome is a significant reduction in overall calorie consumption, as individuals naturally feel less hungry and satisfied with smaller portions. Furthermore, a key benefit is their impact on blood sugar regulation. By stimulating insulin release and inhibiting glucagon (a hormone that raises blood sugar), these drugs help **stabilize blood glucose levels**, which in turn helps curb intense food cravings, particularly for high-sugar or high-carb items. This dual action—satiety control and metabolic improvement—is what makes them so effective for sustained weight management.

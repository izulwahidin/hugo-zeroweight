## Understanding the Calibrate Method: GLP-1s and the Body's Set Point

### The Science Behind GLP-1 Medications for Weight Management

The Calibrate program is fundamentally built upon the science of Glucagon-like Peptide-1 (GLP-1) receptor agonists, a class of medications that have been transformative in the field of metabolic health and weight management. These medications, which include options like semaglutide and liraglutide, work by mimicking a natural hormone produced in your gut when you eat. This hormonal action plays a crucial role in regulating appetite, slowing the rate at which food leaves the stomach (gastric emptying), and promoting feelings of fullness. By influencing these physiological processes, GLP-1s help to effectively 'reset' the body's natural weight set point, which is the weight range your body biologically strives to maintain.

To establish medical authority and credibility for this approach, it is vital to note that GLP-1 medications are **FDA-approved** for chronic weight management in adults with obesity or those who are overweight with a related medical condition. This status is based on extensive, rigorous clinical trial data—such as the landmark STEP trials for semaglutide—which have consistently demonstrated significant and sustained weight loss results well beyond what lifestyle intervention alone can achieve. The science is clear: these medications are not a temporary fix but a powerful tool to address the underlying metabolic dysregulation that makes sustainable weight loss so challenging.

### How Calibrate Clinicians Determine the Right GLP-1 Prescription

The medical component of the Calibrate program is personalized and strictly overseen by the clinical team, which consists of board-certified physicians and other qualified medical professionals. Before any medication is prescribed, the team ensures clinical appropriateness through a comprehensive process. A crucial step involves ordering and reviewing required lab work, which may include tests for markers like Hemoglobin A1c ($HbA1c$) to check for pre-diabetes or diabetes, and Thyroid-Stimulating Hormone ($TSH$) to assess thyroid function.

This data allows the Calibrate clinicians to create a personalized medication plan that considers the member’s complete metabolic profile and health history. Their commitment to appropriate care means that the choice of GLP-1 medication and the titration schedule (the gradual increase in dosage) is tailored to maximize effectiveness while minimizing potential side effects, fully aligning with current standards of obesity medicine care. This rigorous, data-driven approach is essential for demonstrating the high level of medical expertise and quality of care provided by the program.

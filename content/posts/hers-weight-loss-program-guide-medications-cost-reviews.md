Alternatively, Hers may offer **compounded versions** of GLP-1 medications, such as compounded semaglutide or generic liraglutide. For users, it is crucial to understand the distinction:

> **Expert Insight:** The U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) has consistently emphasized that while they oversee the safety and efficacy of **branded, approved GLP-1 drugs**, **compounded drugs** do not undergo the same FDA premarket review for quality, safety, or effectiveness. Compounded versions are typically utilized during national shortages of the brand-name medications but are prepared by state-licensed compounding pharmacies under a healthcare provider’s supervision.

The specific medication prescribed is a clinical decision made by the patient’s dedicated provider after a thorough medical assessment.

### Eligibility Requirements: Who Qualifies for Hers Prescription Treatment?

Prescription weight loss medication, whether oral or injectable, is regulated and not suitable for everyone. Eligibility for the Hers program is determined by established **clinical guidelines** for the safe use of these pharmaceutical treatments.

Generally, to qualify for most prescription weight management medications, including those offered by Hers, patients must meet one of the following criteria, based on major clinical recommendations:

* A **Body Mass Index (BMI) $ \geq 30$** (classified as obesity).
* A **Body Mass Index (BMI) $ \geq 27$** (classified as overweight) *with* at least one weight-related comorbidity, such as high blood pressure (hypertension), Type 2 diabetes, or high cholesterol (dyslipidemia).

It is important to note that the provider will also screen for contraindications—conditions or medications that would make prescription weight loss drugs unsafe, such as a history of certain thyroid cancers (for GLP-1s) or severe depression (for certain oral agents).
